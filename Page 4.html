Manipulation of Time

----- The moment when Daisy arrives after warning her to not bring her husband Tom Buchanan, replying innocently on “Who is Tom” (pg. 83) regarding how long they spent with eachother. Gatsby appears at the door as if it was his first entrance in the storm, meeting Daisy after “Five years next November” (pg. 87), hyper fixated on the time he desires to gain back with her. Fitgerald unfolds on how appearance verses reality comes together, explained by Daisy’s odd response of acting as if she didn’t know who her husband is, defining Tom and Daisy relationship’s Unloyalty behavior among the two social status chasers. With Jay Gatsby’s exact count of how long he has been separated from Daisy’s during the war, presents the fixation of Gatsby wanting to bring back the past, attempting to delete the future where he is not in it. However, during the awkward talk with Daisy and Gatsby, he had dropped an old clock from Nick shelf as he was already embarrassed of meeting Daisy again, calling this a “terrible mistake” (pg. 87) of his. This foreshadows the failed American Dream that Gatsby follows, with the past that he dearly beloves fading away from him, describing the illusions of dreams that society follows.
