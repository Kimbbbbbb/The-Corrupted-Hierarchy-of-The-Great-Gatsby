Gatsby’s Death

----- Despite the death witnessed, Nobody from Nick Carraway vision never stopped to consider the loss of a human soul, with the interpretation as "careless people" (pg. 179) retreating into their wealth. Fitzgerald provides reflection on people’s lack of accountability, forcing the others to deal with the consequences. However, from the kitchen window, Nick senses the “unmistakable air of natural intimacy” (pg. 115), reinforcing their acquaintance even in the unsatisfying marriage between Tom and Daisy. When the summer ends and the fall comes around, Gatsby Mentions that he has “never used that pool all summer” (pg. 162) and wants to enjoy it now. Gatsby’s life fully comes to an end when George found him, as the “holocaust was complete” (pg. 164) when Wilson also took him own. Fitzgerald mixed the comparison of the holocaust, to the domino effect of events accrued to get here, happening all due to human desires and the carelessness of others. Humanity continues to suffer through its own American dream just like Gatsby did, until the cycle repeats again for the future to place more people in a corrupt hierarchy that Jay Gatsby symbolizes as the attractive green light know as wealth.
